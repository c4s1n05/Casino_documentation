01 Purpose

Purpose

This SOP defines the mandatory process for notifying and preparing the Payments Team and Customer Service Team whenever the Marketing Team launches or schedules a promotional campaign. Its goals are to ensure operational readiness, minimise risk exposure, detect bonus abuse early, and deliver a consistent player experience across all touchpoints.

02 Scope

Scope

Applies to all marketing campaigns including but not limited to:

  • Welcome and sign-up bonuses
  • Reload and deposit-match promotions
  • Free spins, free bets, and no-deposit offers
  • Loyalty, VIP, and tiered reward campaigns
  • Seasonal or event-based promotions (e.g. tournaments, jackpots)
  • Referral and affiliate-driven campaigns
  • Retargeting and re-engagement campaigns
03 Roles

Roles & Responsibilities

Marketing Marketing Manager
  • Owns the campaign brief and initiates cross-team notification
  • Ensures the Campaign Brief Pack is complete and distributed on time
  • Monitors for bonus abuse patterns and coordinates cross-team response
  • Holds authority to pause or terminate the campaign if abuse or risk thresholds are breached
  • Chairs pre-launch sign-off and post-campaign debrief meetings
Payments Payments Lead
  • Receives briefing; configures deposit rules
  • Briefs agents on campaign mechanics and escalation path
  • Oversees real-time transaction monitoring
  • Escalates anomalies to Marketing Manager immediately
  • Compiles post-campaign transaction report
Payments Payments Agents
  • Apply campaign-specific deposit rules correctly
  • Monitor higher-value transactions in real time
  • Log and escalate flagged accounts to the Payments Lead
CS CS Lead
  • Receives briefing; trains agents on promotion FAQs
  • Ensures consistent and accurate query resolution
  • Escalates unresolved complaints to Marketing Manager
  • Compiles post-campaign CS query summary
CS CS Agents
  • Answer all player queries on the promotion accurately
  • Follow approved FAQ scripts; never speculate
  • Flag suspected abuse patterns to the CS Lead immediately
  • Escalate complaints and regulatory threats promptly

05 Brief Pack

Campaign Brief Pack

Marketing must produce and distribute a Campaign Brief Pack for every campaign. All sections below are mandatory.

5.1 — General Campaign Information
  • Campaign name and unique reference ID
  • Campaign type (bonus, free spins, referral, reload, etc.)
  • Start date, end date, and active hours with time zone
  • Targeted player segments (new, existing, VIP, geography)
  • Channels used (email, SMS, push notification, paid social, affiliate)
  • Estimated reach and expected uplift in player activity
5.2 — Promotion Mechanics
  • Full Terms & Conditions — plain-English summary and legal version
  • Trigger conditions (minimum deposit, opt-in required, promo code)
  • Bonus value, structure, and award timing
  • Wagering requirements and game restrictions
  • Maximum withdrawal from bonus winnings
  • Expiry periods for bonus credits and free spins
  • Exclusions (payment methods, countries, player segments)
5.3 — Player-Facing Content
  • Approved FAQs and scripted answers for CS agents
  • Links to landing page, banner creative, and email template
  • Key messaging and tone guidance (what to say / what not to say)
  • Escalation wording for players unhappy with the promotion
5.4 — Abuse Risk Parameters (set by Marketing)
  • Defined bonus abuse indicators for this campaign (e.g. structuring deposits, rapid withdrawal post-bonus)
  • Maximum promotional accounts per household or device
  • Payment methods excluded from the promotion
  • Velocity thresholds that trigger a manual review
  • Escalation contact within Marketing for confirmed abuse cases
06 Payments

Payments Team — Specific Responsibilities

6.1 — Pre-Launch Actions
  • Review Campaign Brief Pack and confirm deposit bonus thresholds
  • Configure or verify payment system rules for the promotion period
  • Set up real-time monitoring alerts based on abuse risk parameters from Marketing
  • Brief all Payments agents on campaign mechanics and escalation path
6.2 — During Campaign: Transaction Monitoring

The Payments Team is specifically responsible for monitoring higher-value deposit activity throughout the campaign:

  • Track unusual spikes in deposit volume or average deposit value against baseline
  • Identify players making multiple deposits at or near bonus trigger thresholds (structuring)
  • Flag accounts depositing via multiple payment methods in a short window
  • Monitor for rapid bonus-to-withdrawal patterns
  • Report all flagged activity to the Marketing Manager without delay
6.3 — Escalation Triggers
Transaction volumes exceed [X]% of baseline within a 2-hour window
Notify Marketing Manager immediately via phone and #campaigns-live
Single player depositing above [defined threshold]
Log in Incident Register and notify Marketing Manager within 30 minutes
Confirmed or suspected multi-accounting or promotional abuse
Notify Marketing Manager immediately; Marketing Manager decides on campaign action
NoteMarketing Manager owns all abuse investigations and any decisions to restrict accounts or pause the campaign. All escalations must be logged in the Incident Register within 30 minutes.
6.4 — Post-Campaign
  • Compile transaction volume report for the full campaign period
  • Document all flagged and investigated accounts
  • Submit summary findings to Marketing Manager within 2 business days of campaign end
07 CS Team

Customer Service Team — Specific Responsibilities

7.1 — Pre-Launch Actions
  • CS Lead reviews Campaign Brief Pack and raises ambiguities with Marketing before launch
  • CS Lead drafts or approves the player-facing FAQ document (template from Marketing)
  • Mandatory team briefing held at least 1 working day before launch
  • All CS agents complete a knowledge check on campaign mechanics
  • Escalation contact for complex queries confirmed and circulated
7.2 — During Campaign: Query Handling

CS agents must be equipped to handle all promotion-related player queries, including:

  • How to qualify (eligibility, opt-in steps, promo codes)
  • When and how the bonus or free spins will be credited
  • Wagering requirements and which games count towards them
  • Withdrawal conditions and maximum bonus winnings
  • What to do if a bonus has not appeared as expected
  • Promotion expiry and consequences of not using the bonus in time
  • Why a player may have been excluded from the promotion
CS Golden RuleAgents must never speculate or provide information not in the approved FAQ. If unsure, confirm with the CS Lead before responding. Incorrect information about promotions is an operational and reputational risk.
7.3 — Abuse Indicators: CS Observations

CS agents are often first contact for players attempting to exploit a promotion. Flag the following to the CS Lead immediately:

  • Player enquiring about using multiple accounts to access the promotion
  • Player asking whether they can transfer a bonus to another account
  • Player with a pattern of claiming bonuses and immediately requesting withdrawals
  • Multiple contacts from the same device, IP, or household about the same promotion
  • Player attempting to circumvent an exclusion from the promotion
7.4 — Escalation Triggers
Player has not received bonus after meeting all stated conditions
Escalate to Payments Lead if unresolved within 24 hours
Player alleges the promotion was mis-sold or misleading
Escalate to CS Lead and Marketing Manager
Player threatens a regulatory complaint
Escalate to CS Lead and Marketing Manager immediately; document in full
Promotion-related contacts exceed [X] per hour
CS Lead to notify Marketing Manager; assess whether FAQ update is required
7.5 — Post-Campaign
  • CS Lead compiles summary of promotion-related queries: volume, top issues, resolution rate
  • Flag any recurring ambiguities in campaign T&Cs for future improvement
  • Submit CS summary report to Marketing Manager within 2 business days of campaign end
08 Abuse Mgmt

Bonus Abuse Management (Marketing-Led)

The Marketing Team owns bonus abuse detection and response for all campaigns. Payments and CS feed intelligence upward; Marketing investigates and acts.

8.1 — Abuse Detection Sources
  • Payments Team: transaction monitoring alerts and flagged account reports
  • CS Team: player behaviour observations during live support interactions
  • CRM / BI dashboards: unusual conversion spikes, deposit patterns, and withdrawal velocity
8.2 — Common Abuse Patterns to Monitor
  • Multi-accounting — multiple accounts per player claiming the same bonus
  • Structured deposits designed to trigger bonus thresholds repeatedly
  • Immediate or near-immediate withdrawal following bonus clearance
  • Coordinated group abuse (gnoming) across linked accounts
  • Use of excluded payment methods to circumvent promotion restrictions
  • Arbitrage play — targeting low house-edge games to meet wagering requirements
8.3 — Marketing Response Actions
  • Review flagged accounts against campaign eligibility criteria
  • Void bonuses for accounts in confirmed breach of T&Cs
  • Restrict future promotional eligibility for confirmed abusers
  • Pause or modify campaign parameters if systemic abuse is detected
  • Document all abuse cases in the Abuse Log for pattern analysis
  • Update T&Cs and abuse risk parameters for future campaigns based on findings

10 Incidents

Incident Management

Any operational issue during a campaign — technical fault, unusual player behaviour, abuse pattern, or significant query spike — must be treated as a campaign incident.

1
Detecting team (Payments or CS) logs the incident in the Incident Register immediately
2
Team Lead notifies Marketing Manager via phone and #campaigns-live within 30 minutes
3
Marketing Manager convenes an urgent cross-team call within 1 hour of notification
4
Decision made to continue, modify, or pause the campaign based on severity
5
All decisions and actions documented in the Incident Register
6
Post-incident review held within 3 business days; learnings fed back into this SOP
11 Debrief

Post-Campaign Debrief

A debrief meeting must be held within 5 working days of campaign close. Attendees: Marketing Manager, Payments Lead, CS Lead.

  • Campaign performance vs. targets (impressions, conversions, deposit uplift)
  • Payments review: transaction volumes, flagged accounts, confirmed abuse cases
  • CS review: query volume, top issues, resolution rate, player sentiment
  • Abuse review: patterns detected, actions taken, T&C gaps identified
  • Lessons learned and recommended SOP updates

Debrief notes must be saved to the shared campaign archive. Agreed process improvements must be reflected in this SOP within 10 working days.

12 Version

Document Control & Version History

This SOP must be reviewed and re-approved every 12 months, or immediately following any significant campaign incident. Owner: Marketing Manager.
