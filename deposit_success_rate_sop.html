SOP — Deposit Success Rate Below 80%

Defines the alert system, visibility infrastructure, escalation path and payment team response procedure when the brand's overall deposit success rate falls below 80%. Covers Telegram alert configuration, SoftSwiss escalation and player communication standards.

📅 May 2026
👤 Owner: Payments Team
🔗 Platform: SoftSwiss
⏱ Response SLA: 15 minutes
🚨
Critical Threshold

A deposit success rate below 80% is a critical operational incident. Every 1% drop in success rate represents lost revenue, frustrated players and potential churn. The payment team must respond within 15 minutes of an alert. SoftSwiss must be escalated within 30 minutes if the cause cannot be identified internally. All activity is logged in the incident register and reviewed within 24 hours of resolution.

02
Visibility & Monitoring Infrastructure

Because all payment processing sits with SoftSwiss, the brand does not process transactions directly. Visibility is achieved through SoftSwiss reporting tools, the back office dashboard, and a custom Telegram alert bot that queries the SoftSwiss API on a defined schedule.

ℹ️
Third-Party Payment Infrastructure

SoftSwiss owns the payment stack. The brand has read access to transaction data via the SoftSwiss back office and reporting API. The brand cannot directly modify routing or PSP configuration — all changes must be requested through the SoftSwiss account manager or support portal. This makes the alert system and SoftSwiss escalation process critical tools.

03
Telegram Alert System

A Telegram bot monitors deposit success rate in real time by querying the SoftSwiss API every 5 minutes. All alerts post to a dedicated private group: 🏦 Payments Operations. The group must include the Payments Lead, Payments Team, COO and Marketing Manager (for player comms decisions).

✈️
Telegram Group Setup

Group name: 🏦 [BrandName] Payments Operations  ·  Bot name: @PaymentsMonitorBot  ·  Members: Payments Lead, Payments Team (all), COO, Marketing Manager, Customer Support Lead. This group is ops-only — no external or marketing content. Alerts are automated; human responses and updates are posted manually by the Payments Lead during an incident.

05
Payment Team — Expected Response Actions

The payment team cannot directly fix acquirer or PSP issues — these sit with SoftSwiss. However, the team is expected to rapidly diagnose the issue, communicate clearly, and work with SoftSwiss to drive resolution. The following is the complete set of actions expected from the payment team during an incident.

ActionDetailTool Used
DIAGNOSIS
Identify affected payment methodsPull method-level success rate from SoftSwiss back office. Confirm which methods are failing and which are healthy. This determines whether to recommend alternate methods to players.SoftSwiss Portal
Decode decline reason codesMap decline codes to root causes: Code 05 = Do Not Honour (acquirer); Code 14 = Invalid card; Code 51 = Insufficient funds; Code 54 = Expired card; Code 91 = Issuer not available (technical). Distinguish player-side from acquirer-side declines.SoftSwiss Reports
Check for specific BIN clustersLook for patterns — is the failure rate concentrated on a specific BIN range, card scheme (Visa vs Mastercard) or issuer country? This points toward BIN-level or issuer-level investigation.SoftSwiss BIN Report
Review GEO-level dataPull success rate by player country. A drop concentrated in one GEO suggests an issuer-side or country-specific acquiring problem rather than a platform-wide issue.SoftSwiss GEO Report
Check 3DS failure rate separately3DS failures are separate from acquirer declines. A 3DS failure spike suggests a 3DS provider issue rather than an acquirer issue — different root cause, different SoftSwiss escalation path.SoftSwiss 3DS Report
Check e-wallet success ratesIf card methods are failing but e-wallets are healthy, the team should advise players to use e-wallets via on-site messaging while card issues are investigated. Confirm e-wallet headroom is sufficient for player volume.SoftSwiss Portal
COMMUNICATION
Update Telegram group every 10 minutesManual update from Payments Lead including: current rate, SoftSwiss ticket status, diagnosis progress, any actions taken (rerouting, method adjustment). Be specific — "investigating" is not enough.Telegram
Provide structured brief to SoftSwissWhen raising a ticket, include: timestamp of first detection, current success rate, affected methods, top decline codes with volumes, GEO breakdown, whether 3DS or acquirer, and any pattern noted (e.g. specific BIN cluster).SoftSwiss Portal
Brief Customer SupportSend a clear message to CS lead with: what is happening, which payment methods are affected, what to tell players, and whether e-wallets or other methods are available as alternatives. CS must not give incorrect reassurances.Internal comms
Provide COO incident summaryConcise written summary to COO at 30-minute mark: what happened, current status, SoftSwiss response, estimated time to resolution, revenue impact estimate.Telegram / email
RESOLUTION SUPPORT
Request SoftSwiss rerouting if availableAsk SoftSwiss AM whether traffic can be rerouted to a backup acquirer. Provide decline code data to help them identify the optimal reroute path. Follow up if no response within 15 minutes.SoftSwiss AM (direct)
Verify resolution when rate recoversWhen rate returns above 85%, do not immediately close the incident. Monitor for 15 minutes before declaring resolution. Check per-method rates to confirm all methods have recovered, not just the overall average.SoftSwiss Portal + Bot
Document everything in incident registerLog: time of first alert, time of each escalation step, SoftSwiss ticket reference, cause (as advised by SoftSwiss), resolution action, time to resolution, and revenue impact. This feeds the post-incident review.Incident Register

Player Communications

Player communications are authorised by the COO and delivered by the Marketing Manager. Customer Support must be briefed before any player-facing message goes live. Proactive communication is only issued if the incident exceeds 30 minutes at CRITICAL level or if the support queue sees a significant spike.

09
Post-Incident Review

Every ALERT or CRITICAL incident requires a written post-incident review within 24 hours of resolution. WARNING-level incidents are reviewed if they recur within 7 days.

Review ItemWhat to Document
Incident timelineTime of first alert, time of each escalation step, time of SoftSwiss acknowledgement, time of resolution. Identify any gaps where response was slower than SLA.
Root causeAs confirmed by SoftSwiss — was it an acquirer issue, 3DS issue, SoftSwiss platform bug, or a specific PSP outage? Document the confirmed technical cause, not speculation.
Revenue impactEstimated lost deposits during incident window based on pre-incident hourly deposit rate × duration × failed transaction rate above baseline. Report to COO.
SoftSwiss response assessmentWas SoftSwiss response within agreed SLA? Was the AM reachable? Was the resolution appropriate? Log formally — used in quarterly SoftSwiss SLA review.
Alert system performanceDid the Telegram bot fire at the right time? Were thresholds correct? Any false positives or missed alerts? Update bot configuration if needed.
Team response assessmentWere all escalation steps followed in the correct order and within SLA? Were communications to COO, CS and players timely and accurate?
Prevention recommendationsWhat could be done to prevent recurrence? E.g. request SoftSwiss add additional backup acquirer, increase monitoring frequency for a specific method, add new alert threshold.
SOP update required?Does this incident reveal a gap in this SOP? If yes, update SOP within 5 business days and note the change in the document control log.
